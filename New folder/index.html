Parasite

Parasite (Korean: 기생충; RR: Gisaengchung) is a 2019 South Korean dark comedy[8] thriller film directed by Bong Joon-ho, who co-wrote the screenplay with Han Jin-won and co-produced. The film, starring Song Kang-ho, Lee Sun-kyun, Cho Yeo-jeong, Choi Woo-shik, Park So-dam, Jang Hye-jin, Park Myung-hoon, and Lee Jung-eun, follows a poor family who infiltrate the life of a wealthy family.

Dialogue

Jalel Lantud

"Parasite" is a Korean film directed by Bong Joon-ho that tells a compelling and darkly humorous story about class struggle and family dynamics. The film begins with the Kim family, who are struggling to make ends meet while living in a small, semi-basement apartment. They engage in low-paying, temporary jobs and are constantly looking for a way to improve their situation. One day, the son, Ki-woo, gets a lucky break when his friend recommends him for a well-paying tutoring job for the wealthy Park family. Ki-woo is hired to tutor the Park family's daughter, Da-hye, and he quickly sees an opportunity for his entire family to infiltrate the Park household. Through a series of clever manipulations and deceitful schemes, each member of the Kim family secures a job with the Parks, pretending not to know each other. Ki-jung, Ki-woo's sister, becomes an art therapist for the Parks' young son, Da-song. Their father, Ki-taek, is hired as the Park family's driver, and their mother, Chung-sook, replaces the Parks' long-time housekeeper. The Kims revel in their new positions and the comfort and luxury of the Parks' beautiful home. However, their scheme begins to unravel when the former housekeeper, Moon-gwang, returns to the house, revealing a shocking secret. She and her husband have been secretly living in a hidden basement bunker. Tensions rise as the Kims and Moon-gwang's family clash, leading to a series of violent and chaotic events. The story culminates in a dramatic and tragic ending, where the stark contrast between the lives of the rich and the poor is laid bare. The film explores themes of greed, social inequality, and the lengths people will go to in order to escape poverty. "Parasite" is a poignant, thought-provoking, and masterfully crafted film that leaves a lasting impact on its audience.
